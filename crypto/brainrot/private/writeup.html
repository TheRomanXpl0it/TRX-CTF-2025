Brainrot - Writeup

We're given three equations and need to recover a flag, which we can split into five parts:

f0,f1,f2,f3,f4 f_0, f_1, f_2, f_3, f_4 f0​,f1​,f2​,f3​,f4​

These equations represent the evaluation of a polynomial at specific points. The unknowns (the flag parts) are the coefficients of this polynomial. The given values are just different points where the polynomial has been evaluated:

P(x)=∑i=04rot8000(fi)⋅xi P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{4} \mathrm{rot}_{8000}(f_i) \cdot x^i P(x)=i=0∑4​rot8000​(fi​)⋅xi

We also have two modulus values that play a role in how the results are structured:

m1=b2l(b′cant_give_you_everything′) m_1 = \text{b2l}(b'\text{cant\_give\_you\_everything}') m1​=b2l(b′cant_give_you_everything′)
m2=b2l(b′only_half!!!′) m_2 = \text{b2l}(b'\text{only\_half!!!}') m2​=b2l(b′only_half!!!′)

The Given Equations

The challenge gives us three polynomial evaluations, but with a double modulo operation applied. First, the result is reduced modulo m1m_1m1​, and then that result is further reduced modulo m2m_2m2​:

P(0xdeadbeef)mod  m1mod  m2=r1P(13371337)mod  m1mod  m2=r2P(0xcafebabe)mod  m1mod  m2=r3 \begin{aligned} P(0x\text{deadbeef}) \mod m_1 \mod m_2 &= r_1 \\ P(13371337) \mod m_1 \mod m_2 &= r_2 \\ P(0x\text{cafebabe}) \mod m_1 \mod m_2 &= r_3 \end{aligned} P(0xdeadbeef)modm1​modm2​P(13371337)modm1​modm2​P(0xcafebabe)modm1​modm2​​=r1​=r2​=r3​​

The function rot8000\mathrm{rot}_{8000}rot8000​ applies a specific transformation that encodes each flag part using UTF-16. It can be expressed as:

rot8000(’FLAG’).encode(′utf−16′)=0xfffe0000000000000000+∑i=03((0x40+FLAG[i])×256+0x1f)×2562(3−i) \mathrm{rot}_{8000}(\text{'FLAG'}).encode('utf-16') = 0xfffe0000000000000000 + \sum_{i=0}^{3} ((0x40 + \text{FLAG}[i]) \times 256 + 0x1f) \times 256^{2(3-i)} rot8000​(’FLAG’).encode(′utf−16′)=0xfffe0000000000000000+i=0∑3​((0x40+FLAG[i])×256+0x1f)×2562(3−i)

(This can be recovered just by playing with this function a bit)

Working Around the Moduli

To simplify things, we introduce two helper variables k1k_1k1​ and k2k_2k2​:

eq−k1⋅m1−k2⋅m2=res \text{eq} - k_1 \cdot m_1 - k_2 \cdot m_2 = \text{res} eq−k1​⋅m1​−k2​⋅m2​=res

Since k2k_2k2​ is roughly m1/m2m_1 / m_2m1​/m2​, we end up with a system of three equations where the solutions are small and bounded.

Solving with LLL

At this point, solving the equations comes down to finding small solutions to a linear system. This is exactly what LLL is good for.
